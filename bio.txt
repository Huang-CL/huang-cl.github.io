Dr. Chenliang Huang obtained his B.S. in Physics from Peking University in 2011 and received his PhD in Astronomy from the University of Virginia in 2017. After that, he was a postdoc at University of Nevada, Las Vegas from 2017 to 2019 and Lunar and Planetary Laborartory, University of Arizona from 2019 to 2022.  He joined Shanghai Astronomical Observatory as an associate research professor since 2023.  His research interest includes the study of the upper atmosphere of gas giant exoplanets, the interior structure of terrestrial exoplanets, and radiative transfer in interacting supernova remnants.
